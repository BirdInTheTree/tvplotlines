A popular U.S. senator and presidential candidate collapses at a fundraiser, and Vogler assigns House to the high-profile case. Vogler offers to let House keep his entire team if he endorses Vogler's pharmaceutical company in a speech. The senator's symptoms initially suggest AIDS, but House investigates deeper. House deduces that Chase is Vogler's informant within his team. During his required speech, House insults Vogler and his company instead of endorsing them, reigniting their conflict. Cameron quits the team in response to the ongoing political pressure. Meanwhile, House treats a woman who claims to be pregnant without having sex. The senator is diagnosed with toxoplasmosis and delayed-onset CVID secondary to phenytoin-mediated Epstein-Barr virus infection.
