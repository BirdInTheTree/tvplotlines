A young patient won't stop bleeding after a car accident with his girlfriend. House accepts Cuddy's challenge to go off Vicodin for a week in exchange for no clinic duty for a month. As House's withdrawal symptoms intensify, his diagnostic methods become increasingly harsh and risky, causing Foreman and Cameron to worry about his judgment. Despite his impaired state, House solves the case by exhuming the family's recently deceased cat and performing an autopsy. He discovers high levels of naphthalene, which termites excrete as a repellent, revealing that the patient has been exposed to poisonous vapors from a termite nest behind his bedroom walls.
