Foreman believes an uncooperative homeless woman is faking seizures to secure food and shelter at the hospital. Wilson becomes invested in her case, determined to prevent her from falling through the healthcare system's cracks. House uses the case to teach two medical students about conducting patient histories. The team ultimately diagnoses the woman with both tuberculoma and rabies, but the rabies is too advanced for treatment and she dies. During treatment, the patient bites Foreman, requiring him to undergo rabies prophylaxis. Feeling remorseful for doubting the patient's condition, Foreman and Wilson search for her family and discover she became homeless after losing her husband and son in a car accident two years earlier. To give her peace as she dies, Foreman pretends to be her deceased husband and offers her forgiveness.
