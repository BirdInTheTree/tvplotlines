Rebecca Adler, a 29-year-old kindergarten teacher, collapses from a seizure in her classroom and loses her ability to speak. Dr. Gregory House initially refuses the case until Dr. James Wilson claims Rebecca is his cousin. When Dr. Lisa Cuddy tries to force House to fulfill his clinic duties, he refuses and has his MRI authorization revoked as punishment. House diagnoses Rebecca with cerebral vasculitis and begins treatment, which initially improves her condition. To investigate the source of her seizures, House convinces Dr. Eric Foreman to break into Rebecca's house. At the hospital, Rebecca suddenly loses her vision and suffers another seizure, contradicting House's diagnosis. Foreman discovers ham in Rebecca's house, revealing Wilson's lie—Wilson is Jewish, and the presence of ham suggests Rebecca is not his cousin. The ham discovery leads House to suspect tapeworms as the cause. When Rebecca refuses treatment for neurocysticercosis, House persuades her by having Dr. Robert Chase perform a non-invasive X-ray that reveals a tapeworm larva in her leg, confirming their diagnosis. Rebecca agrees to treatment and recovers.
