House informs Chase and Foreman that Cameron has quit. A pregnant woman arrives with brain and kidney problems, and the team discovers she has small cell lung cancer. She and her husband must choose between her life and their unborn child's survival. Vogler escalates his campaign against House by proposing the board revoke House's tenure and fire him, but Wilson opposes the motion. Vogler then proposes removing Wilson from the board, which passes, prompting Wilson to resign in protest. At the subsequent meeting, Cuddy challenges Vogler's control over the board, and four other members support her, forcing Vogler to leave as chairman and revoke his $100 million donation. The team celebrates Vogler's departure, though Cuddy reminds House the conflict was unnecessary. House also treats a malnourished baby whose vegan parents are arrested for child endangerment, but a CT scan reveals the baby has DiGeorge syndrome, exonerating the parents.
