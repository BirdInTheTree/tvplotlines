Dan, a 16-year-old high school lacrosse player, begins experiencing night terrors and hallucinations. His parents bring him to House after receiving a letter that Dr. Allison Cameron sent in House's name. Upon meeting the family, House starts a bet with his team about whether the parents are Dan's biological relatives. Dan's condition worsens with myoclonic twitches and a blocked blood vessel, leading House to make an initial diagnosis. When Dan suffers an auditory hallucination during a procedure, House's diagnosis is ruled out. House secretly performs an unauthorized paternity test using the parents' coffee cups and discovers neither parent is biologically related to Dan. This revelation triggers House to recall a previous case involving vaccination refusal, leading him to theorize that Dan contracted a measles virus during childhood. House confirms subacute sclerosing panencephalitis with a retinal biopsy and successfully treats Dan.
