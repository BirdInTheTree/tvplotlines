Sister Augustine arrives at the hospital with severe hand rashes that her fellow nuns believe resemble stigmata. House diagnoses contact dermatitis from dish soap allergy and prescribes antihistamines. The medication triggers an asthma attack, prompting House to administer epinephrine, which causes Augustine to suffer a minor heart attack. House's team suspects he made an error with the epinephrine dosage, but Augustine develops new symptoms including hallucinations, convulsions, and leg rashes. Cuddy removes House from the case due to his controversial methods. Investigation of the convent reveals figwort tea, which explains only the epinephrine reaction. Despite being placed in a hypoallergenic room, Augustine continues having allergic reactions. When she begins shouting about having God inside her, House realizes she has a copper IUD that's causing the allergic responses. The device is surgically removed and Augustine fully recovers.
