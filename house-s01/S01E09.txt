John Henry Giles, a legendary jazz musician with ALS, is admitted for pneumonia treatment under Foreman's care. House attempts to prove John Henry doesn't actually have ALS, but his intervention causes respiratory failure. House intubates John Henry in violation of his DNR order and uses a legal technicality to keep him on life support. Cameron discovers a blood clot, which is removed, and an MRI reveals an arteriovenous malformation. Surgery to correct the malformation restores John Henry's ability to walk, proving House's theory that he never had ALS. Meanwhile, Foreman receives a lucrative job offer from John Henry's doctor, who is Foreman's former mentor, creating a professional dilemma.
