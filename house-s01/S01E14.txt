Billionaire entrepreneur Edward Vogler donates $100 million to Princeton-Plainsboro and becomes Chairman of the Board. Vogler plans to transform the clinic into a profitable biotech venture and eliminate House's financially draining department. A successful businesswoman arrives with inexplicable paralysis despite her seemingly perfect life and health. House diagnoses her with congestive heart failure caused by bulimia and regular ipecac use, but treating her puts his job and medical license at risk due to Vogler's opposition to his methods and department.
