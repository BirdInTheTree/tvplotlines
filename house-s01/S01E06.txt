Lucille Palmeiro collapses when a blood clot travels from her leg to her heart. At the hospital, she begins vomiting blood, leading House to suspect vitamin K deficiency. The team discovers unused ampicillin and frozen microwave dinners in her home, supporting House's diagnosis. An ultrasound reveals liver cirrhosis and a cancerous tumor too large for surgical removal. House orders ethanol injection to shrink the tumor, making surgery possible, but the team cannot explain the cirrhosis. Lucille makes an unexpected decision to call Social Services to remove her son, which House claims is inconsistent with her supposed schizophrenia. The team considers Wilson's disease as an explanation for all symptoms, and an eye exam reveals copper-colored rings around her irises, confirming the diagnosis. Lucille receives treatment for Wilson's disease, recovers completely, and reunites with her son.
