House diagnoses Mark, Stacy Warner's husband, who presents with stomach pain while tests show no clear condition, though his brain appears to be deteriorating. House initially finds abdominal epilepsy but cannot detect memory loss. When Mark develops paralysis, House treats him for Guillain-Barré syndrome. After confiding in Stacy that he still has feelings for her, House realizes Mark has been experiencing delusions and actually suffers from acute intermittent porphyria. Despite opposition from his team, House administers a dangerous drug cocktail to confirm the AIP diagnosis, with Stacy's support. The treatment succeeds, and Mark recovers. Cuddy decides to hire Stacy as the hospital's lawyer, setting up ongoing complications for House.
