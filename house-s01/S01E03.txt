Brandon, a college student, collapses after having sex with his fiancée and presents with numerous seemingly unrelated symptoms. Foreman and House propose competing diagnoses, each arguing their theory better follows Occam's razor. When Brandon's white blood cell count drops dramatically, both doctors' theories are proven wrong. At the clinic pharmacy, House theorizes that the pharmacist accidentally dispensed colchicine instead of cough medicine, which would explain all of Brandon's symptoms except his persistent cough. House administers the antidote and Brandon begins recovering immediately, though the pharmacist denies making any mistake. When Brandon mentions his old cough medicine didn't have letters on the pills like his current medication, House discovers colchicine pills that closely resemble cough medicine tablets, confirming the accidental poisoning and validating his diagnosis.
