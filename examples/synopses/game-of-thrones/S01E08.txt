Syrio Forel, Arya Stark's Braavosian sword master, fights off Lannister guards sent to arrest her, allowing Arya to escape the Red Keep while her sister Sansa Stark is captured. News of Ned Stark's imprisonment reaches Winterfell, prompting Robb Stark to call the Northern banners and prepare for war against the Lannisters. Robb leaves his younger brother Bran Stark behind to rule Winterfell in his absence as he marches south with the Northern armies. Catelyn Stark travels to the Eyrie seeking military support from her sister Lysa Arryn, but Lysa refuses to commit the Vale's forces against the Lannisters, forcing Catelyn to leave and join Robb's war camp instead. Meanwhile, Tyrion Lannister and his sellsword Bronn are ambushed by Hill Tribes while traveling through the Mountains of the Moon. Tyrion convinces the tribesmen to spare their lives by promising them greater rewards if they escort him safely to his father. At Tywin Lannister's military camp, the Lord of Casterly Rock meets with tribal leader Shagga and offers the Hill Tribes substantial payment and weapons in exchange for their service against the Starks. King Joffrey Baratheon appoints his grandfather Tywin as the new Hand of the King, consolidating Lannister power in King's Landing. Sansa pleads with Joffrey to show mercy to her father Ned, and the young king agrees to spare Ned's life on the condition that he publicly confesses to treason and acknowledges Joffrey's legitimate claim to the Iron Throne. At Castle Black, Jon Snow and Samwell Tarly retrieve corpses of rangers killed beyond the Wall, but one of the bodies reanimates as a wight and attacks Lord Commander Jeor Mormont before Jon destroys it with fire. Across the Narrow Sea in Essos, Khal Drogo's khalasar raids nearby settlements to capture slaves for sale, using the profits to fund their planned invasion of Westeros. During one of these raids, Drogo sustains a wound while fighting an enemy warrior, though he initially dismisses the injury as minor.
