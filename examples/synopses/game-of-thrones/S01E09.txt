The Stark army arrives at the Twins, where Catelyn Stark negotiates with Lord Walder Frey for passage through his lands. Frey agrees to let them cross and provide military support, but demands that Robb Stark marry one of his daughters and Arya Stark marry one of his sons in the future. Catelyn accepts these terms to secure the alliance. At the Wall, Lord Commander Jeor Mormont presents Jon Snow with Longclaw, his family's Valyrian steel sword, as a reward for saving Mormont's life from the wight attack. In Essos, Khal Drogo's infected wound worsens and he falls into a fever, leaving him near death. Desperate to save her husband, Daenerys Targaryen asks Mirri Maz Duur, a maegi and former slave, to perform blood magic to heal Drogo. Mirri warns that only death can pay for life and instructs everyone to stay outside the tent during the ritual. As the spell begins, Daenerys goes into premature labor. Jorah Mormont, panicking, carries her into the tent where the blood magic is taking place, despite Mirri's warnings. Robb Stark executes his battle plan against the Lannister forces, deliberately sacrificing two thousand men in a frontal assault as a diversion. While the Lannisters focus on this attack, Robb leads his main force around to capture Jaime Lannister, successfully taking him prisoner. In King's Landing, Ned Stark appears before the Great Sept of Baelor for a public confession. Following the deal negotiated by Varys and others, Ned falsely confesses to treason and publicly declares Joffrey Baratheon the rightful king, expecting to be spared and sent to the Night's Watch. However, King Joffrey ignores the agreement and orders Ned's immediate execution. Despite protests from Sansa Stark, Queen Cersei, and his advisors, Joffrey commands Ser Ilyn Payne to behead Ned. The execution proceeds, shocking the crowd and the nobles who expected mercy.
