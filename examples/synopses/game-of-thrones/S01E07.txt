Ned Stark confronts Queen Cersei Lannister in the godswood, revealing he knows her children were fathered by her brother Jaime, not King Robert Baratheon. Ned warns Cersei to flee King's Landing with her children before he tells Robert the truth. King Robert returns from a hunting trip mortally wounded by a boar and summons Ned to his deathbed. Robert dictates his final will, naming Ned as Lord Protector and regent until Prince Joffrey comes of age. However, Ned deliberately writes 'my rightful heir' instead of Joffrey's name, knowing the boy is illegitimate. After Robert dies, Ned sends a message to Stannis Baratheon, Robert's brother and the true heir to the Iron Throne. In the throne room, Ned presents Robert's will to the court and declares Joffrey has no claim to the throne. Queen Cersei tears up the document and orders Ned's arrest for treason. Ned had arranged for Petyr 'Littlefinger' Baelish to secure the loyalty of the City Watch, but Baelish betrays him at the crucial moment. The gold cloaks turn on Ned's men, killing several and capturing Ned himself. At Castle Black, First Ranger Benjen Stark leads a ranging party beyond the Wall to investigate reports of wildling activity, but the group fails to return. Lord Commander Jeor Mormont assigns the new recruits to their orders within the Night's Watch. Jon Snow receives appointment as Mormont's personal steward rather than a ranger as he had hoped. Samwell Tarly also becomes a steward, assigned to Maester Aemon. Sam suggests to Jon that Mormont chose him as steward to groom him for future leadership of the Watch. In Essos, an assassin disguised as a wine merchant attempts to poison Daenerys Targaryen with a bottle of poisoned wine. Ser Jorah Mormont grows suspicious of the merchant's behavior and stops Daenerys from drinking. The assassin flees but is captured by Dothraki warriors. Under interrogation, he confesses that King Robert hired him to kill the Dragon Queen. Khal Drogo, enraged that someone tried to harm his pregnant wife, declares he will cross the narrow sea and conquer the Seven Kingdoms for his unborn son. Drogo orders his khalasar to march toward the coast to begin their invasion of Westeros.
