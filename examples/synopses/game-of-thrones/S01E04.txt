Tyrion Lannister arrives at Winterfell and presents Bran Stark with blueprints for a special saddle that will allow him to ride despite his paralysis. Maester Luwin examines the design and confirms it will work, giving Bran hope for the first time since his fall. Tyrion departs for King's Landing but stops at an inn at the crossroads. Catelyn Stark unexpectedly appears at the same inn, having left Winterfell in secret. She recognizes Tyrion and publicly accuses him of providing the catspaw assassin with the Valyrian steel dagger used in the attempt on Bran's life. Catelyn calls upon her father's bannermen present in the inn, including knights from House Bracken and House Blackwood, who arrest Tyrion despite his protests of innocence. In King's Landing, Ned Stark continues investigating Jon Arryn's death by visiting the armorer Tobho Mott. The armorer reveals that Jon Arryn had come to see Gendry, one of King Robert's bastard sons, shortly before his death. Ned observes that Gendry has the distinctive Baratheon black hair and strong build, unlike the golden-haired royal children. At the Wall, Samwell Tarly arrives as a new recruit to the Night's Watch, immediately becoming a target for the other trainees due to his cowardice and soft upbringing as a lord's son. Jon Snow intervenes when the recruits brutally beat Sam during training, threatening Rast and the others with his direwolf Ghost if they continue the abuse. Jon takes Sam under his protection, helping him find ways to contribute to the Watch despite his inability to fight. In Essos, Viserys Targaryen grows increasingly frustrated with his diminished status as Daenerys gains confidence and authority among the Dothraki. He confronts his sister about her transformation, demanding she remember that he is the rightful king and she must obey him, but Daenerys no longer fears her brother's threats and begins to assert her independence from his control.
