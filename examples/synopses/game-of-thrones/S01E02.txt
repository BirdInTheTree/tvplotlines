Ned Stark, newly appointed Hand of the King, departs Winterfell for King's Landing with his daughters Sansa Stark and Arya Stark. Catelyn Stark remains behind to care for her unconscious son Bran Stark, who lies in a coma after his fall. An assassin with a Valyrian steel dagger enters Bran's chamber and attempts to kill the boy, but Catelyn fights him off despite suffering cuts to her hands. Bran's direwolf Summer kills the assassin, saving both Catelyn and Bran. Catelyn becomes convinced that House Lannister orchestrated the assassination attempt, deepening her suspicions about Bran's original fall.

Jon Snow, Ned's illegitimate son, begins his journey north to the Wall to join the Night's Watch, accompanied by his uncle Benjen Stark. Tyrion Lannister, Queen Cersei's dwarf brother, decides to travel with them to see the legendary Wall for himself. The group faces the harsh realities of the road as they head toward their destination.

Along the Kingsroad toward King's Landing, Prince Joffrey Baratheon encounters Arya practicing sword work with her friend Mycah, a butcher's boy. Joffrey draws his sword and threatens the commoner boy, prompting Arya to defend her friend by hitting the prince with a stick. When Joffrey turns his blade on Arya, her direwolf Nymeria attacks and bites the prince's arm. Arya throws Joffrey's sword into the river, and she and Mycah flee into the woods. Arya later releases Nymeria into the wild to protect her from royal retribution.

King Robert Baratheon holds court to judge the incident, with Queen Cersei demanding justice for the attack on her son. Despite Arya's truthful testimony, Sansa Stark refuses to contradict Joffrey's lies, claiming she cannot remember what happened. Cersei insists that a direwolf must die for the attack, and when Nymeria cannot be found, she demands that Sansa's direwolf Lady be executed instead. Against Ned's protests, Robert orders the killing, and Ned personally executes Lady to spare her a crueler death. The royal party later discovers that Joffrey's men have killed Mycah.

Across the Narrow Sea, Daenerys Targaryen continues adapting to her new life as Khal Drogo's wife among the Dothraki. She receives guidance from Ser Jorah Mormont, an exiled Westerosi knight who has joined her service, as she learns Dothraki customs and works to earn her husband's respect and affection.
