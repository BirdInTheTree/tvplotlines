Following Ned Stark's execution, the North declares independence from the Seven Kingdoms and crowns Robb Stark as King in the North. With Jaime Lannister captured and held prisoner by the Starks, the political landscape shifts dramatically. Both of Robert Baratheon's brothers challenge young King Joffrey's legitimacy—Stannis Baratheon and Renly Baratheon each stake their own claims to the Iron Throne, fracturing the realm into multiple factions. Recognizing the mounting threats to Joffrey's rule, Tywin Lannister makes a strategic decision to appoint his son Tyrion as acting Hand of the King while Tywin himself leads military campaigns to defend the crown. At the Wall, Jon Snow struggles with conflicting loyalties and attempts to abandon his Night's Watch vows to join his brother Robb's war and avenge their father's death. His fellow brothers-in-arms intervene and convince him to honor his sacred oath to the Watch. Lord Commander Mormont then assigns Jon to join an expedition beyond the Wall to search for his missing uncle Benjen Stark, giving Jon a mission that serves both duty and family. In King's Landing, Yoren, a Night's Watch recruiter, orchestrates Arya Stark's escape from the capital by disguising her as a boy and smuggling her out before she can be used as a political pawn. Meanwhile, King Joffrey announces his intention to make Sansa Stark his queen despite having executed her father, using her as both a trophy and a tool to legitimize his claim to northern loyalty. Across the Narrow Sea, Daenerys Targaryen faces devastating personal losses when her child is born deformed and stillborn, the result of Mirri Maz Duur's blood magic ritual that was supposed to save Khal Drogo's life. Instead, Drogo survives but remains in a vegetative state, leaving Daenerys with an impossible choice. She ends her husband's suffering by smothering him, then prepares his funeral pyre according to Dothraki custom. Daenerys places her three dragon eggs on Drogo's burning pyre and has Mirri tied to the flames as punishment for her treachery. Despite Ser Jorah Mormont's desperate pleas, Daenerys walks into the fire herself. When dawn breaks and the ashes cool, Daenerys emerges unharmed from the ruins, accompanied by three newly-hatched dragons—the first seen in the world for over a century. Jorah and the remaining Dothraki witnesses kneel before her, recognizing her as something beyond mortal.
