Varys the Spider informs King Robert Baratheon that Daenerys Targaryen is pregnant with Khal Drogo's child. Robert immediately orders her assassination, viewing the unborn child as a future threat to his throne. Ned Stark, serving as Hand of the King, refuses to sanction the murder of a pregnant girl and argues that Daenerys poses no immediate danger across the Narrow Sea. When Robert insists on the assassination, Ned resigns his position as Hand in protest and prepares to return to Winterfell. Meanwhile, Catelyn Stark arrives at the Eyrie with her prisoner Tyrion Lannister, presenting him to her sister Lysa Arryn, who rules the Vale as regent for her young son Robin. Catelyn accuses Tyrion of attempting to murder her son Bran, seeking justice from her sister. Lysa, still grieving her husband Jon Arryn's death and paranoid about Lannister threats, agrees to hold Tyrion for trial. News of Tyrion's capture reaches King's Landing, enraging the Lannister family. Jaime Lannister confronts Ned Stark in the streets, demanding his brother's immediate release. Ned explains that he had no part in Tyrion's capture, but Jaime refuses to believe him. The confrontation escalates into violence as Jaime draws his sword and attacks Ned. The two men duel briefly until one of Jaime's soldiers stabs Ned in the leg from behind, ending the fight dishonorably. Wounded and unable to continue fighting, Ned collapses as Jaime expresses disgust at his man's interference. Jaime declares his intention to ride north and retrieve his brother personally, leaving Ned bleeding in the street. The incident marks a complete breakdown in relations between House Stark and House Lannister, pushing the realm closer to open conflict.
