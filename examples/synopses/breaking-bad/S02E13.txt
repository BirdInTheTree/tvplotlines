Jesse wakes up next to Jane's body and screams, calling 911 in hysterics. Paramedics arrive and pronounce Jane dead. Jesse is inconsolable, and when Walt arrives at the scene — feigning shock — Jesse collapses against him. Walt, suppressing his guilt, tells Jesse he is sorry and arranges through Saul Goodman to have Jesse admitted to a rehabilitation facility before the police can question him further. Saul's fixer Mike Ehrmantraut, a former police officer who handles Saul's sensitive operations, arrives at Jesse's house and cleans it of all drug evidence before the detectives process the scene. Jane's death is ruled an accidental overdose. Jane's father, Donald Margolis, an air traffic controller, is destroyed by his daughter's death. He returns to work too soon, visibly grief-stricken and unable to concentrate. Walt undergoes surgery to remove his remaining tumor, and Skyler drives him to the hospital. In the pre-operative prep room, Walt, groggy from anesthesia, nearly confesses the truth about his second phone and his double life, mumbling incoherently while Skyler listens with growing alarm. The surgery succeeds, and Walt recovers in the hospital. Skyler visits and tells Walt that she is relieved the surgery went well, but her manner is cold and distant. She has pieced together enough inconsistencies — the second phone, the lies about the Schwartzes, the unexplained absences — to know that Walt has been systematically deceiving her, though she does not yet know about the meth. Jesse enters rehab and begins the slow process of confronting his addiction and his grief over Jane. Walt returns home from the hospital to find his family waiting. He retrieves the bag of cash from the crawl space under the house and stares at it. A flash-forward connects to the season's opening image: Donald Margolis, Jane's father, returns to his air traffic control station and, distracted by grief, fails to issue a correction to two aircraft on converging paths. Wayfarer Flight 515, a Boeing 737, collides midair with a King Air charter plane directly above Albuquerque. Debris rains down across the city, and a pink teddy bear — the same one seen in the pool in the season's opening — falls from the sky into the White family's backyard. The collision kills 167 people. Walt watches the news coverage from his living room. The chain of causation is complete: Walt let Jane die, which broke her father, whose lapse caused the crash. Skyler tells Walt she wants him to move out.
