Jesse Pinkman and Walter White's methamphetamine operation begins gaining traction in Albuquerque's drug market. DEA agent Hank Schrader starts investigating reports of a new high-quality product, unaware that his brother-in-law Walter is the manufacturer he seeks. At a White family barbecue, Walter decides to reveal his lung cancer diagnosis to his son Walt Jr., sister-in-law Marie Schrader, and Hank. Hank immediately promises Walter that he will always protect and provide for the White family. Walt Jr. becomes angry and upset, confronting his father about acting as if everything is normal despite the devastating news. Jesse grows increasingly paranoid about law enforcement attention and decides to visit his estranged middle-class family. His parents, Adam and Diane Pinkman, react with disappointment and concern when they see Jesse's deteriorated appearance and erratic behavior. During the visit, Jesse discovers that his younger brother Jake, who excels in academics, athletics, and music, has been secretly smoking marijuana to cope with their parents' intense expectations and pressure. When the parents find the marijuana, Jesse chooses to protect Jake by allowing them to believe it belongs to him. The parents force Jesse to leave the house, reinforcing his status as the family disappointment. Desperate and isolated, Jesse approaches Walter to argue that they should resume their methamphetamine production partnership. Walter firmly rejects Jesse's proposal and tells him never to contact him again, attempting to sever their criminal relationship. Walter and his wife Skyler meet with an oncologist to discuss treatment options for his cancer. The doctor explains that Walter's condition is treatable with aggressive therapy. Skyler feels hopeful about the prognosis and pushes for immediate treatment. Walter expresses serious doubts about pursuing treatment, citing the prohibitive financial costs and questioning whether the family can afford the medical expenses that would be required.
