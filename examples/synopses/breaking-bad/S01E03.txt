Marie Schrader mistakenly believes her nephew Walter Jr. is smoking marijuana and asks her DEA agent husband Hank Schrader to intervene. Hank takes Walter Jr. to a rundown motel frequented by drug addicts and prostitutes, using the squalid environment to lecture him about gateway drugs and their consequences. Walter White and Jesse Pinkman continue cleaning up the aftermath of their confrontation with Emilio's crew, disposing of Emilio's remains while keeping Domingo 'Krazy-8' Gallardo chained in Jesse's basement. Walter struggles with the decision of whether to kill Krazy-8 or find another solution, torn between his moral reservations and the practical danger of keeping him alive. While bringing food to the captive dealer, Walter collapses on the basement stairs and smashes the plate, scattering ceramic shards across the floor. When Walter regains consciousness, he reveals his lung cancer diagnosis to Krazy-8—the first person he has told about his condition. The two men begin an unexpected conversation about their lives, discovering shared experiences and commonalities that humanize their captor-prisoner relationship. Krazy-8 exploits this moment of vulnerability to plant seeds of doubt about Jesse, warning Walter that drug addicts are unreliable partners who will inevitably betray him. Convinced by their conversation and his own reluctance to commit murder, Walter decides to release Krazy-8 and begins cleaning up the broken plate fragments. While disposing of the ceramic pieces, Walter realizes that one shard is missing and deduces that Krazy-8 has hidden it as a makeshift weapon. Walter confronts Krazy-8 about the missing shard, and Krazy-8 immediately attacks him with the concealed ceramic blade. Walter defends himself by strangling Krazy-8 with the bicycle lock that had been restraining him, killing the dealer in a desperate struggle. Walter returns home and lies to his wife Skyler White about working late at the car wash, but Skyler reveals she has already spoken with his former boss and knows he quit his job weeks earlier. Caught in his deception and overwhelmed by the day's events, Walter tells Skyler they need to have a serious conversation.
